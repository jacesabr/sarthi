02 · The real problem

A kid fails quadratics for reasons that aren't quadratics.

The crack is never where the lesson is. It's three layers down — a shaky grasp of factoring, fractions, or negative signs.

No teacher with 200 kids has time to find it. So the gap compounds — year after year.

03 · Why this wasn't possible before

A private tutor used to mean a private tutor.

Until 2024, personalised coaching meant hiring a human — hours, scheduling, salary.

In 2026, AI is finally good and cheap enough to give every student their own. First time in history.

04 · What we built

Sarthi. First-principles learning.

Sarthi talks with the student. The moment they stall, it traces the gap downward — concept by concept — until it hits something they already know. Then it teaches its way back up — by conversation, not lecture.

05 · Teach, test, climb

You only climb when you've proven it.

No stale lesson plans. Sarthi teaches by conversation — Socratic, back-and-forth — until it actually clicks. Then the student has to prove it before moving up.

Prove every level back to the top → the topic's done, and the next one unlocks.

06 · Why we start here

The only exam where 100% mastery is an honest promise.

JEE has no syllabus ceiling — "complete mastery" is a lie. Class 10 is the opposite: one book, one syllabus, a finite set of papers.

  • 14 Math + 13 Science chapters. That's the whole game.
  • ~9 sample papers + ~60 board papers — all with marking schemes.
  • A finite corpus means we can hit 100% of it. No other exam lets you say that.

Win a bounded corpus, then expand.

07 · Why now

Three lines crossed this year.

  • AI got cheap. ~₹100/month per student now — 10× cheaper than 2023, impossible in 2021.
  • Phones reached the kids. Mobile use among kids 5–14 jumped 17% → 67% (2020→2023); youth 15–29 sit at ~95%.
  • Parents got tired. ₹2 lakh for a 2% chance? The math finally broke.

11 · How it works

A cheap agent at every point. One brain on top.

Each concept = its own cheap, cached agent. One frontier director watches the map and decides when to go deeper — so the expensive model barely runs.

12 · Why it compounds

Cheaper and better the more it's used.

Most AI faces a tradeoff: more users → higher cost → cheaper model → worse answers.

Sarthi runs that tradeoff backwards.

  • Lessons self-hone. Every kid who struggles teaches the system a better way to explain — saved and reused.
  • Answers are banked. The questions and explanations that work are already stored, so the costly model runs less, not more.

A chatbot answers the question. Sarthi knows the explanation that's worked for 10,000 kids before you.
