02 · The real problem

A kid fails quadratics for reasons that aren't quadratics.

When a student gets stuck, the crack is almost never where the lesson is. It's three layers down — a shaky grasp of factoring, or fractions, or negative signs. No teacher with 40 students has time to trace it, so the gap gets papered over — and it compounds, chapter after chapter, year after year.

Every student needs someone who will stop, find the exact brick that's missing, rebuild from there, and not move on until they've proven it. That has never been affordable. Until now.

03 · Why this wasn't possible before

A private tutor used to mean a private tutor.

Until 2024, "personalised coaching" meant hiring a human. Hours, ego, scheduling, salary.

As of 2026, AI is good enough and cheap enough to give every Indian student their own tutor — for less than a Domino's pizza per month. This is the first time in history this has been possible.

06 · Why we start here

The only exam where 100% mastery is an honest promise.

JEE has no syllabus ceiling — there's always a harder question, so "complete mastery" is a lie. CBSE Class 10 is the opposite: one NCERT textbook, one official syllabus, a finite bank of past papers. The corpus is bounded and definitive.

  • 14 Math chapters, 13 Science chapters. That's the whole game — nothing outside the book is ever asked.
  • ~9 official sample papers + ~60 board-paper variants — every one backed by an official marking scheme.
  • Because the corpus is finite, we can take a student to 100% of it — and prove it. No other exam lets you say that.

Win a bounded corpus completely, then expand to where the corpus is bigger.

11 · How it works

A cheap agent at every point. One brain on top.

Each concept is taught by its own small, cached agent. A single frontier director watches the whole map and decides when to drop a level — so the expensive model barely runs, and every lesson gets cheaper as the agents learn.

12 · Why it compounds

Every student we teach makes the next lesson better.

The code isn't the moat — the data is. Two assets compound, and neither can be copied overnight:

  • Verified concept graphs. Mapping a subject to its prerequisite chain and checking every link is slow, expert curriculum work. Once built, it's ours.
  • A per-concept teaching flywheel. We store every conversation, mine the explanations and "aha" moments that actually got a student past the gate, and feed them back into that concept's tutor — so each one gets measurably better at teaching its one thing.

A general chatbot can answer the question. Only Sarthi knows, for this exact concept, the explanation that has worked for 10,000 kids before you.
